Grab some friends, and become a Host!

Who is a Host?

A host is any individual - parent, teacher, coach, campus minister, youth pastor, small group leader - anyone who loves to memorize and study God’s Word with others.

If you have a heart to share the Word of God, this is a simple, easy, and practical way to journey through the Scriptures. No prior knowledge or experience necessary!

We understand that hosts have different time commitment levels. We want to work with you right where you are. It can be as simple as using the Bible Bee study resource, or as involved as encouraging young people interested in competing on a national level.

What do Hosts do?

  • Introduce the Bible Bee study resources into their group/home.
  • Provide a connection point between Bible Bee Headquarters and students. The team at headquarters cannot personally connect with every student who uses the study. But we can connect with you, and help you connect with the students you influence!
  • We highly recommend regular meeting times for your group. As we hear repeatedly from hosts and participants - the best part of the Bible Bee is the community - and hosts offer that opportunity! A Bible Bee study without the community just isn’t the same. If you can’t find a host to connect with, grab a friend and start your own group!

Additional
opportunities include:

Helping students compete on a national level! All you have to do is facilitate the local online test for students, and listen to their oral Scripture recitation.

Receiving financial assistance to plan a special local awards celebration. (Must have minimum number of contestants registered).

What do we provide for Host?

  • An information packet with all the information you will need.
  • Bible Bee study resources - that teaches students how to unlock the Scriptures for themselves.
  • Support from other Hosts.
  • Financial resources for awards and supplies - including fundraising support and ideas.
  • Games, crafts, and ways to make learning and memorization fun!

What is the Calendar for the Summer Program?

  • August 6Local Test Day
  • August 13Local Awards Ceremony (National contestant qualifiers are announced)
  • November 16-20The National Bible Bee Competition
